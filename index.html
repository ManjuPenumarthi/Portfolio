AI-Assisted NetSuite Development: Evaluating LLMs and Building a Budget-Variance Chatbot

ARTIFACT 01

As a Senior NetSuite Developer at Corey Construction, a residential and commercial roofing company, I've been incorporating AI-assisted development into my day-to-day work — using tools like ChatGPT and Claude with structured prompt engineering to accelerate SuiteScript development and documentation. This artifact captures a deeper, deliberate version of that practice: comparing multiple AI tools on a real NetSuite scripting task, stress-testing a custom GPT's reasoning through iterative dialogue, using an AI research assistant, and ultimately designing and deploying a functional chatbot using design thinking.

Description A multi-part project where I: (1) compared ChatGPT, Claude, and Copilot on writing a SuiteScript budget-check script — catching a real syntax bug and assumption errors that would have failed in production; (2) worked with a custom coding GPT through six rounds of iterative refinement on a Purchase Order approval model, pushing it to justify its assumptions and catching where it self-corrected; (3) used STORM AI, an AI research tool, to investigate construction ERP job costing and evaluated its source-grounded research process; and (4) applied design thinking (Empathy → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test) to plan, build, and deploy a working chatbot that answers real budget-variance questions using NetSuite-style data.
Objective To critically evaluate multiple generative AI tools on real, domain-specific technical tasks, and to apply structured design thinking to build a functional AI assistant — demonstrating both technical judgment and a repeatable process for evaluating AI output rather than trusting it blindly.
Process I used my own NetSuite/construction background to test each AI tool against tasks where I could independently verify correctness. This included comparing code architectures across three LLMs, running a six-step structured dialogue with a custom GPT (change / add info / ask for more / ask why / what-if / summarize), evaluating an AI research tool's multi-perspective methodology, and following a full design-thinking cycle to scope, plan, build, and test a chatbot — including debugging a real deployment issue around data persistence across conversations.
Tools & Tech ChatGPT (Business Pro), Claude, Microsoft Copilot, STORM AI, Chatbase.co, SuiteScript 2.1, NetSuite job-costing concepts
Value Proposition This artifact shows I can work with AI tools as a developer should — verifying output against domain expertise rather than accepting it at face value — while also being able to translate a real business pain point into a working AI solution using a structured design process.
Unique Value Most people either use AI tools uncritically or avoid them out of skepticism. I demonstrated a middle path: rigorous evaluation combined with practical application, specifically within a niche domain (construction ERP/NetSuite) where generic AI knowledge often falls short.
Relevance AI-assisted development is already part of my role at Corey Construction, and this artifact shows the more rigorous version of that practice — the kind of critical tool evaluation and structured problem-solving I bring to production ERP work, not just coursework.
